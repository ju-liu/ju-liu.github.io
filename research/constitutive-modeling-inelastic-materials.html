Constitutive modeling of inelastic material behavior

This research direction focuses on building robust constitutive models for inelastic solids under complex loading paths. We are interested in thermo-mechanical coupling, rate dependence, anisotropy evolution, and path-dependent dissipation mechanisms.

Key topics

  • Finite-strain viscoelastic and viscoplastic formulations
  • Internal-variable based constitutive laws for cyclic loading
  • Damage and failure indicators for soft and engineering materials
  • Parameter identification and uncertainty quantification
  • Algorithmic implementation for large-scale finite element simulations

Methodological emphasis

We develop physically motivated and mathematically consistent constitutive frameworks with careful attention to thermodynamic admissibility, objectivity, and numerical stability. On the computational side, we emphasize efficient stress update algorithms and scalable implementations for high-performance computing environments.

Applications

Applications include soft biological tissues, polymer-like materials, and other inelastic media relevant to medical devices and multiphysics simulations.
