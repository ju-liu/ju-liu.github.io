I am an assistant professor working at SUSTech, Department of Mechanics and Aerospace Engineering.

My research employs mathematical models and large-scale computations to model the behavior of complex multi-physics problems in engineering and biomedicine. Applications include vascular and cardiac fluid-structure interactions, mechanical and biological behavior of soft tissues, and liquid-vapor phase transitions. My research work has been awarded the Robert J. Melosh medal from Duke University, and I have been invited to present at the Stanford Fluid Mechanics Seminar. I am looking for doctoral and master students with strong programming skills and good background in mechanics and mathematics. Please send me an email with your CV if you are interested

  • A computational framework for fluid-structure interaction
  • A computational framework for isogeometric analysis of visco-hyper-elastodynamics
